Projects

Modeling the Evolution of Retina Neural Network

Independent Research
Dr. Paul Munro

Retina neural network is highly conservative. How was it evolved? Are there any effective alternatives? Answering these questions can give insights into artificial neural network. To model the evolution of retina neural network, I proposed an efficient way to encode it and adapted the genetic algorithm, which leads to biologically plausible phenomena. Based on the results, I discussed the potential impacts of evolutionary programming to artificial neural network.

Analyzing and Modeling Mouse Trajectories during Olfactory Navigation

Independent Research
Dr. Nathan Urban   Urban Lab

Ofaction is commonly used in nature to guide navigation. What information about decision and/or learning during olfactory navigation do mouse trajectories convey? To answer this question, I attempted to analyze the mouse trajectories with machine learning and model the behaviors using multiple approaches, such as multi-order HMM and modified infotaxis.

Short Presentation | BSOiD Analysis Report (Jupyter-Notebook)

09/2019 - 07/2020

Modeling Neural Plasticity with Multisensory Braitenberg Vehicle

Independent Research
Dr. Bradly Alicea   Orthogonal Research & Education Lab

Braitenberg vehicle is an agent that respond automatically to the environment. It is interesting to develop a Braitenberg vehicle that behaviorally manifests neural plasticity. I proposed and constructed a Hebbian Braitenberg vehicle that associates the stimuli of one sense with the “preference” of another sense in a virtual two-sensory environment.

Code & Detailed Descriptions

Reconstruction of Visual Patterns From V4 Firing and Local Field Potentials

Selected Class Project
Dr. Aaron Batista & Dr. Bistra Iordanova   BIOE1586 - Quantitative Systems Neuroscience

Inspired by Neural Population Control via Deep Image Synthesis by Bashivan et al., where the team synthesized patterns that selectively control the activity level of macaques’ V4 subpopulation utilizing artificial neural network, I proposed to perform the "reverse-engineering"---apply generative adversarial network to reconstruct seen visual patterns from macaques’ V4 spiking activities and local field potentials.

NIH Grant Proposal (Exercise)

Generative Model for Visual Storytelling with Stick Figures

Selected Class Project
Dr. Adriana Kovashka   CS1699 - Deep Learning

One hot topic in deep learning is how to enable neural network to encode and generate character relationships. My colleagues and I proposed several versions of stacked generative adversarial networks to randomly create series of moving stick figures that tell stories via their bodily interactions. We implemented the models, though not getting promising enough results.
